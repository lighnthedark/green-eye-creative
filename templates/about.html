The Mission

Teach One.

Reach One.

This isn't just about building apps. It's about proving something — to myself, and to everyone who looks like me, who came up like me, who was told the window had closed.

I am a self-taught developer who didn't start writing code until after 20 years in another career. No bootcamp. No CS degree. No shortcuts. Just a decision — and then the daily discipline to back it up. 8 to 15 hours a day on these projects. Not because anyone is watching. Because that's what the work requires.

If you're reading this and you think it's too late for you — it's not. The seed doesn't care how long it sat in the ground before it broke through. It just needs the right conditions and the will to grow.

That's the mission. Teach one. Reach one. Build something real and show others the way.

Philosophy

Ubuntu

"I am because we are."

Ubuntu is an ancient African philosophy — the belief that no person rises alone. That every achievement is built on the shoulders of those who came before, and carries a responsibility to lift those who come after.

Every product I build carries that weight. Every line of code is dedicated to three generations who worked so I could be here — and to the next generation watching to see if it's possible.

It's possible.

The Journey

2024

He crossed waters so his children would not have to. That work ethic — that refusal to quit — runs all the way through to right now.
